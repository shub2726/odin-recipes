Egyptian Karkade Tea

An Infusion of Dried Calyces of the Roselle Flower

This Hibiscus Iced Tea is consumed in Egypt, Sudan and other parts of Africa as well, Middle East and Latin America. Its names may differ from country to country such as Bissap in Senegal, Agua de flor de Jamaica, Agua de Jamaica and Rosa de Jamaica in Mexico, Central America, and parts of South America and Sorrel in Caribbean Jamaica.

Its thirst-quenching effect makes Karkade an excellent option for replacing aerated drinks in the hotter areas.

Ingredients

  • Â½ cup Hibiscus flowers dried
  • Lime Juice
  • 5 Cups Water
  • Mint Leaves

Steps

  1. Firstly, bring water to a boil. Add the dried Hibiscus flowers.
  2. Then place Karkade concentrate in the refrigerator for 1-2 hours to cool.
  3. Just before serving, add some chilled water. Take care not to dilute it too much. Taste the Karkade. Accordingly add more chilled water and sugar syrup. Adding sugar syrup is optional. Alternatively, you could add maple syrup or honey.
