Mawa Cake

The dense, moist, cardomon-flavoured Mawa Cake

Mava Cake, originally a Parsi dessert, has gained widespread popularity. The adoration for this mawa cake is further boosted by the fact that mawa is a crucial ingredient in the majority of festive Indian sweets and desserts. Additionally, the development of eggless baking has made it more practical for Indian households to indulge in festive bakes.

Ingredients

  • Mawa
  • Sugar
  • Baking Powder
  • Milk Powder
  • Salt
  • Butter
  • Milk
  • Curd
  • Dry Nuts
  • Cardomom Powder

Steps

  1. Firstly, in a bowl sift all-purpose flour along with salt, baking powder and baking soda. Mix thoroughly.
  2. Secondly, grind sugar along with a few saffron strands and some cardamom seeds.
  3. Beat butter in a mixing bowl till creamy.
  4. Add curd and mix. Now add milk powder.
  5. Pour batter into a 8” greased and lined round baking tin.
  6. Sprinkle some chopped nuts on top.
  7. Bake in a preheated oven at 160°C for 35 to 45 minutes.
  8. Check doneness by inserting toothpick into the center of cake. If it comes out clean, the cake is baked. Otherwise bake for a few minutes more.
